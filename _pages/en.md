# 📑 Research Projects

### Large-scale Complex Capacitated Vehicle Routing Problem

- We modeled the problem as a capacitated vehicle routing problem with multiple parking lots, multiple drop-off points, and multiple trips based on real-world datasets. A two-stage algorithm was applied: a greedy-based initial solution generation algorithm in the first stage, and a memetic algorithm with a custom search operator for further optimization in the second stage.

### Vehicle Trajectory Prediction Problem

- In highly interactive autonomous driving scenarios, we extract features from semantic information (such as traffic rules) provided by high-precision maps using neural networks, and predict the trajectories of surrounding vehicles under traffic rule constraints.

### Driving Scenario Generation

- We developed a cost-effective autonomous driving scenario generation method that can generate driving scenarios from scratch, including diverse road networks, traffic rules, roadside structures, and dynamic traffic flow, validated in multiple popular simulators.

### Controllable Multimodal Vehicle Motion Behavior Generation

- We proposed a framework for generating vehicle motion behavior that achieves both controllability and multimodality, comprising initial vehicle position generation, controllable multimodal trajectory candidate generation, and trajectory selection. Experiments demonstrate strong controllability, multimodality, and generalization capability.

### Interactive Driving Scenario Generation Platform

- We developed an interactive driving scenario generation platform that supports users in generating and visualizing diverse driving scenarios at a fine-grained level, covering road networks and motion trajectories.

### Co-evolution of Autonomous Driving Scenarios and Prediction Models

- Due to limited real-world data that cannot fully reflect traffic complexity, we aim to improve trajectory prediction model performance and robustness through automatic scenario generation co-evolving with the models.
